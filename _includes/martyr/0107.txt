THE bringing back of the Child Jesus from Egypt. 
&mdash;The same day, the birthday of blessed Lucian, 
a priest of the church of Antioch and martyr, who 
was distinguished for his learning and eloquence. 
He suffered at Nicomedia for the confession of 
Christ, in the persecution of Galerius Maximian, 
and was buried at Helenopolis, in Bithynia. His 
praises have been proclaimed by St. John Chrysostom.&mdash;At Antioch, St. Clerus, deacon, who, for having professed faith in Christ, was seven times tortured, a long while kept in prison, and at length 
ended his martyrdom by decapitation.&mdash;In the city 
of Heraclea, the holy martyrs Felix and Januarius. 
&mdash;The same day, St. Julian, martyr.&mdash;In Denmark, St. 
Canute, king and martyr. His feast is celebrated on 
the 19th of this month.&mdash;At Pavia, St. Crispin, 
bishop and confessor.&mdash;In Dacia, St. Nicetas, bishop, 
who made fierce and barbarous nations humane and 
meek by preaching the Gospel to them.&mdash;In Egypt, 
St. Theodore, a saintly monk, who flourished in the 
time of Constantine the Great. He is mentioned by 
St. Athanasius, in his Life of St. Anthony.&mdash;At Barcelona, St. Raymond of Pennafort, of the Order of 
Preachers, celebrated for sanctity and learning. 
His festival is kept on the 23d of this month.
